# Email Sequence Intake Checklist

This checklist defines what must be known before copywriting begins, what is optional, what is risky to assume, what can be inferred, and what should trigger a red flag.

Use this to validate intake briefs and determine readiness for strategy and copywriting phases.

---

## MUST KNOW (Blocks Copywriting)

These 8 fields are non-negotiable. If any are missing, copywriting cannot responsibly begin.

- [ ] **Business/Client Name** — Who are we writing for?
- [ ] **Offer Definition** — What exactly is being sold (product/service name, core mechanism, price range)?
- [ ] **Sequence Goal** — What is this sequence trying to achieve? (e.g., "Drive webinar registration", "Close software trial upgrade")
- [ ] **Target Audience** — Who are we writing to? (job title, company type, or ICP at minimum)
- [ ] **Primary Pain Point** — What problem does this solve? (must be specific, not generic)
- [ ] **Desired Outcome** — What transformation or result does the buyer want?
- [ ] **Primary CTA** — What specific action do we want them to take?
- [ ] **Funnel Stage** — Where in the customer journey does this sequence sit? (awareness / consideration / decision / retention / reactivation)

**Gate Rule:** If any of these 8 are blank or vague, request clarification before proceeding to copywriting.

---

## OPTIONAL (Improves Quality)

These 8 fields significantly improve output quality and persuasiveness. Gather them during strategy phase if not available.

- [ ] **Historical Performance Data** — What did similar sequences achieve before? (open rates, CTR, conversion rate)
- [ ] **Proof Assets** — Case studies, testimonials, data, certifications, or results available?
- [ ] **Brand Story/Origin** — Why does this business exist? (humanizes and builds connection)
- [ ] **Competitor Positioning** — How is this different from alternatives?
- [ ] **Objection Research** — What prevents people from buying? (specific objections, not generic doubts)
- [ ] **Buying Triggers** — What events/circumstances make someone ready to buy now?
- [ ] **Decision-Making Process** — How do their customers buy? (solo decision / team / multiple approvals)
- [ ] **Brand Voice Guidelines** — Tone, style, messaging pillars already defined?

**Quality Gate:** For each missing optional field, flag it as "to be researched during strategy phase" rather than guessing.

---

## RISKY ASSUMPTIONS (Flag Explicitly)

These 7 items represent high-risk assumptions. If any are being assumed, document them and require stakeholder sign-off.

1. **Assuming ICP without validation**
   - Risk: Wrong audience messaging = poor response
   - Required: At minimum, confirm job title and company size

2. **Assuming primary pain without research**
   - Risk: Solving wrong problem = no response
   - Required: Confirm this is their #1 pain (or top 3 ranked)

3. **Assuming objection handling without input**
   - Risk: Overcommunicating about non-concerns / missing real objections
   - Required: Ask what prevents them from buying TODAY

4. **Assuming offer differentiation exists**
   - Risk: Generic messaging = commodity positioning
   - Required: Confirm at least 1 meaningful differentiator

5. **Assuming performance benchmarks without data**
   - Risk: Setting unrealistic KPI targets
   - Required: Get historical open/click/conversion rates or admit no benchmark exists

6. **Assuming compliance/claims are substantiated**
   - Risk: Legal liability, platform violation, FTC issues
   - Required: Confirm claims have proof or remove them

7. **Assuming buyer is aware of solution**
   - Risk: Wrong messaging approach (education vs. persuasion)
   - Required: Confirm their awareness state (unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / offer-aware)

**Red Flag Process:** For each assumption, document it in the intake brief and require explicit stakeholder acknowledgment.

---

## CAN BE INFERRED (During Strategy Phase)

These 8 items can be researched and inferred by a research or strategy team if not provided. Do not delay copywriting pending these.

1. **Competitive Positioning** — Research competitor messaging, positioning, pricing
2. **Industry Best Practices** — Research what works in this vertical
3. **Audience Psychology** — Research buying behavior, decision criteria, trends
4. **Social Proof Opportunities** — Identify gaps in proof assets that can be created
5. **Messaging Angles** — Develop core message framework from research
6. **Email Performance Benchmarks** — Industry benchmarks if historical data unavailable
7. **Regulatory Requirements** — Legal/compliance research for the industry
8. **Persona Deep-Dive** — Detailed psychographic and behavioral research

**Process:** Create a "Strategy Research Checklist" with these items. Assign them to research phase, not copywriting phase.

---

## RED FLAGS (Requires Stakeholder Review)

These 10 situations warrant explicit stakeholder review and sign-off before copywriting begins.

- [ ] **Unsubstantiated Medical/Health Claims** — Any health claims without third-party proof (doctor, research, etc.)
- [ ] **Offer Not Fully Defined** — Price/positioning/delivery method unclear
- [ ] **Conflicting Goals** — Sequence goal conflicts with company strategy or other sequences
- [ ] **Regulatory Risk** — Compliance concerns flagged but not resolved
- [ ] **No Proof Assets** — Zero case studies, testimonials, or results available
- [ ] **Target Audience Mismatch** — ICP poorly defined or likely to exclude high-value customers
- [ ] **Unrealistic KPI Targets** — Targets exceed industry benchmarks by 3x+ with no justification
- [ ] **Multiple Contradictions** — Audience pain contradicts offer benefits, or messaging pillars conflict
- [ ] **Brand Voice Undefined** — No guidance on tone, no examples, no guardrails
- [ ] **Legal/Internal Approval Unclear** — No clarity on who approves copy before send

**Red Flag Process:** For each flag present, document it and require written sign-off from appropriate stakeholder (legal, compliance, CMO, etc.) before copywriting commences.

---

## WARNINGS (Document Risk Assumptions)

These 10 situations are not blockers but require explicit documentation of assumptions and risks.

- [ ] **First Sequence for This Offer** — No performance history; results are uncertain
- [ ] **Early-Stage Product** — Offer not yet market-tested; positioning may shift
- [ ] **New Target Audience** — Company entering new market; buyer behavior unknown
- [ ] **Competitive Market** — Saturated vertical; differentiation unclear or weak
- [ ] **Limited Proof Assets** — Few case studies or testimonials; claims harder to substantiate
- [ ] **Tight Timeline** — Limited strategy/research time; copy may be based on incomplete data
- [ ] **Multiple Decision-Makers** — Complex buying process; messaging must address multiple personas
- [ ] **High Price Point** — Requires more objection handling and proof
- [ ] **Unproven Audience Channel** — Email list quality/responsiveness unknown
- [ ] **Long Sales Cycle** — Sequence may need to nurture for extended period; engagement will decline naturally

**Warning Process:** For each warning present, add a note to intake brief: "ASSUMPTION: [specific assumption] — Risk: [consequence if wrong] — Mitigation: [how we'll test/monitor]".

---

## QUALITY GATES (Validate Before Copywriting)

These 4 gates ensure the brief is mature enough for high-quality copywriting.

### Gate 1: Information Completeness
- [ ] All 8 "Must Know" items are filled in and specific (not vague)
- [ ] No more than 2 of the 8 "Optional" items are missing
- [ ] Decision: **PASS** / **NEEDS CLARIFICATION**

### Gate 2: Assumption Clarity
- [ ] All risky assumptions documented and ranked by risk level
- [ ] Stakeholder acknowledges top 3 risks and accepts them
- [ ] Decision: **PASS** / **NEEDS STAKEHOLDER SIGN-OFF**

### Gate 3: Red Flag Resolution
- [ ] All red flags addressed or explicitly accepted
- [ ] Written approval from relevant stakeholder on each flag
- [ ] Legal/compliance cleared if applicable
- [ ] Decision: **PASS** / **BLOCKED PENDING RESOLUTION**

### Gate 4: Strategic Alignment
- [ ] Sequence goal aligns with broader marketing strategy
- [ ] Target audience is optimal for business goals
- [ ] Proof/messaging assets exist or can be created in timeline
- [ ] KPI targets are realistic and measurable
- [ ] Decision: **PASS** / **NEEDS STRATEGY ALIGNMENT**
