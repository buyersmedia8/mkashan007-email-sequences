# Email Sequence Intake Skill

## Purpose

This skill systematically collects the minimum viable brief needed to write high-converting email sequences. It refuses to guess when critical inputs are missing and structures outputs for downstream research, strategy, and copywriting skills.

## Usage

Invoke this skill when beginning any new email sequence project. It will:

1. Gather all required business and marketing context
2. Identify gaps in critical information
3. Flag assumptions and risks
4. Recommend if brief is ready for strategy/research phase
5. Output a structured intake document ready for copywriting

## Inputs

The skill accepts information in any format:
- Natural language briefing
- Existing marketing collateral
- Customer research notes
- Competitor analysis
- Historical campaign data
- Ad copy samples

## Process

### 1. Information Collection Phase

The skill will systematically ask for or extract:

**Business Foundation**
- Client/business name
- Industry/vertical
- Current business stage

**Offer Definition**
- What is being sold (product/service)
- Core mechanism/mechanism of action
- Price (or price range)
- Delivery method (digital, physical, hybrid)
- Key benefits vs. competitors

**Sequence Context**
- What goal is this sequence trying to achieve
- What funnel stage does it address (awareness, consideration, decision, retention, reactivation)
- How does this fit into broader marketing strategy

**Audience**
- Ideal customer profile (ICP) - demographics, psychographics, behaviors
- Where they currently are (aware/unaware of solution)
- Primary job title and decision-making role
- Key challenges they face

**Psychology & Motivation**
- Top 3-5 pain points this offer solves
- Desired outcomes/transformation they seek
- What prevents them from buying today
- Common objections to address
- Belief barriers to overcome

**Differentiation**
- What makes this offer different
- Key differentiators vs. competitors
- Unique positioning angle
- Proof assets available (case studies, testimonials, data, certifications)

**Messaging & Brand**
- Brand voice (authoritative, friendly, technical, simplified, etc.)
- Tone preference (urgent, educational, consultative, provocative)
- Brand story or origin if relevant
- Messaging pillars if they exist

**Risk & Compliance**
- Regulatory concerns (medical, financial, FTC, GDPR, etc.)
- Claims that need substantiation
- Banned or risky angles for this industry
- Internal compliance review requirements

**Campaign Parameters**
- Desired sequence length (number of emails)
- Email frequency (daily, every-other-day, weekly, etc.)
- Primary KPI to optimize for (open rate, click rate, conversion rate, revenue per email)
- Secondary KPIs
- Known benchmark data or historical performance

**Constraints**
- Budget limitations
- Timeline pressures
- Internal stakeholder constraints
- Technology/platform limitations
- Content assets already created that must be used

### 2. Validation Phase

For each collected input, validate:
- Is this information specific enough to act on?
- Are there contradictions or unclear elements?
- What assumptions would we be making if this field was left blank?

### 3. Risk Assessment Phase

Flag:
- Missing information that creates high risk
- Assumptions that should be validated before copywriting
- Compliance red flags
- Unrealistic expectations
- Contradictions in brief
